The Going Away

Leaving home for college in an unfamiliar place is both exciting and overwhelming. It’s a bittersweet goodbye to the familiar and a leap into the unknown, filled with uncertainty and the promise of new beginnings. For many, it’s the first real taste of independence—a chance to carve out a space in the world and discover who they are beyond the boundaries of their childhood home.

The days leading up to departure are often a whirlwind of emotions. You’re busy packing boxes, buying dorm essentials, and saying goodbye to friends and family. Each item you pack feels like a small piece of your life being uprooted. That favorite book you always kept on your nightstand, the photos of high school friends that line your walls, or the mug you always used for your morning coffee—they all hold memories of the life you’re about to leave behind.

And then there’s the goodbye itself. Hugging your parents, siblings, or childhood friends for the last time before you leave feels like a tug-of-war between excitement and sadness. For parents, it’s letting go of the child they’ve raised, and for students, it’s stepping away from the comfort of everything they’ve ever known. But as hard as the farewell may be, there’s an underlying sense of hope—hope for the experiences, friendships, and growth that await.

Arriving at college is a sensory overload. The campus feels massive, even if it’s relatively small, and there are people everywhere. Everyone seems to be moving with purpose while you’re still trying to figure out which building is which. Your dorm room, at first glance, might feel stark and impersonal, but as you unpack and add your own touches—a blanket from home, photos of loved ones, or fairy lights—it slowly starts to feel like your space.

Orientation week is an adventure in itself. You’re thrown into a sea of new faces, trying to remember names and majors, and participating in icebreakers that range from awkward to genuinely fun. There’s a sense of camaraderie as everyone is in the same boat, navigating the unfamiliar together. But it’s not all easy. Homesickness can hit when you least expect it. Maybe it’s a quiet moment in your dorm, a holiday that feels emptier without family traditions, or even a meal that doesn’t taste like the ones from home. These pangs of longing are normal, and over time, they’re balanced by the joys of new friendships, exploring the campus, and discovering hidden gems in your new city.

Academics also add to the adjustment. College courses can be a step up in difficulty, and managing your time becomes more important than ever. Without the structure of high school and the watchful eyes of parents, you’re in charge of your own schedule. It can feel daunting, but it’s also empowering. You learn to prioritize, to ask for help when needed, and to find your rhythm.

One of the most exciting parts of leaving home for college is the opportunity for self-discovery. You’re exposed to new perspectives, cultures, and ideas that challenge your worldview and broaden your horizons. You meet people from all walks of life, each with their own stories and aspirations, and these interactions help shape the person you’re becoming. Clubs, organizations, and activities give you the chance to explore your interests and passions, or even discover new ones you never knew you had.

Over time, the unfamiliar becomes familiar. The campus that once felt enormous starts to feel like home. The people you met during orientation become friends you can’t imagine life without. Your dorm transforms into a place of comfort, and the city that seemed so daunting starts to feel like yours to explore. Leaving home for college is undoubtedly a big step, but it’s a step worth taking. It’s a journey of growth, resilience, and discovery. You may stumble along the way, but each challenge is an opportunity to learn and adapt. And as you navigate this new chapter, you realize that while you’ve left home, you’re also creating a new one—one built on your own terms, with memories and experiences that will stay with you forever.

So, if you’re about to take that leap, embrace it with open arms. Let the excitement carry you forward, let the challenges teach you, and let the new beginnings inspire you. Because this journey—this mix of excitement, fear, and wonder—is what makes leaving home for college such a transformative experience.
